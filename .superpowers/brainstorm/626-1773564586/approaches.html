Plot Feature — Three Approaches

How the empty state + wizard should work together. Click the one that feels right.

A

Teach First, Then Build

Empty state: A warm, illustrated explainer fills the main content area. Shows a visual diagram of what a 3-act structure looks like with a sample story. Explains acts, beats, and how they help shape a narrative.

CTA: "Ready to structure your story?" → opens wizard modal

Wizard: Step 1: Pick a template (3 cards with illustrations). Step 2: Review the acts + pre-filled beats. Step 3: Customize names/descriptions. Done.

Beats: Pre-populated from template (e.g. "Inciting Incident", "Midpoint Reversal"). Author edits or removes what doesn't fit.

Pro: Most educational — authors learn before committing.
Con: Slower to first action. Empty state needs good visual design to not feel like a wall of text.

B

Template Cards as Empty State

Empty state: A brief warm intro ("Every great story has a structure. Let's find yours.") followed by 3 beautiful template cards directly in the main area. Each card shows the template name, a one-line description, and a mini visual of the act structure.

CTA: Clicking a template card opens the wizard modal

Wizard: Step 1: Confirm template (already chosen). Step 2: Walk through each act one at a time — show the act name, its beats, and ask "What happens in your story here?" Step 3: Summary view. Done.

Beats: Shown per-act during the walkthrough. Each beat has a brief mentor-like prompt ("This is where your character's world changes...").

Pro: Combines teaching + action. Template cards ARE the education. Per-act walkthrough feels personal.
Con: Less upfront explanation of what acts/beats even are.

C

Hybrid: Intro → Cards → Wizard

Empty state: Two sections. Top: A compact explainer with a warm illustration and 2-3 sentences about what story structure is and why it helps. Bottom: The 3 template cards to pick from.

CTA: "Choose a structure to get started" — clicking a card opens the wizard

Wizard: Step 1: Template overview (acts laid out visually). Step 2: Per-act customization with pre-filled beats and mentor prompts. Step 3: Review & create. Done.

Beats: Pre-filled from template with warm descriptions. Author can rename, reorder, or remove. Each beat has a tooltip/helper explaining its purpose.

Pro: Best of both worlds — teaches without blocking, then gets to action fast. Empty state stays compact.
Con: Slightly more UI to design. The intro section needs to be brief enough to not feel like homework.
